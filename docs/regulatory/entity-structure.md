| Precedent | Structure | Equity Split | Regulatory Status |
|-----------|-----------|--------------|-------------------|
| **Zodia Custody** | Separate UK Ltd. | SCV ~90%, Management ~10% | FCA-registered custodian |
| **Zodia Markets** | Separate entity | SCV majority | FCA-registered, MAS-licensed |
| **Libeara** | Separate entity | SCV majority | HKMA Supervisory Sandbox participant |

**AoaBank follows identical pattern**: separate Pte. Ltd. with own ACRA registration, own MAS license application, independent board, and ring-fenced balance sheet.

## 2. ACRA Registration Requirements Checklist

Registration with the Accounting and Corporate Regulatory Authority (ACRA) of Singapore for a Private Limited Company (Pte. Ltd.).

### 2.1 Pre-Registration Requirements

| # | Requirement | Status | Notes |
|---|-------------|--------|-------|
| 1 | **Company Name Approval** | ☐ Pending | Reserve "AoaBank Pte. Ltd." via BizFile+. Name must not be identical/similar to existing registered names. Check against ACRA name database. |
| 2 | **Minimum 1 Shareholder** | ☐ Pending | SC Ventures entity (majority ~90%) + Execution Team holdco/individuals (~10% ESOP). Max 50 shareholders for Pte. Ltd. |
| 3 | **Minimum 1 Director (Singapore Resident)** | ☐ Pending | At least one director must be a Singapore citizen, permanent resident, or EntrePass/EP holder. The SCV Venture Lead or a locally-resident nominee can fulfil this. |
| 4 | **Company Secretary** | ☐ Pending | Must appoint within 6 months of incorporation. Must be a natural person ordinarily resident in Singapore. Typically outsourced to corporate secretarial firm. |
| 5 | **Registered Address** | ☐ Pending | Physical address in Singapore (not P.O. Box). Co-working/virtual office acceptable for initial period. Must be operational during business hours. |
| 6 | **Share Capital** | ☐ Pending | Minimum S$1 paid-up capital (no minimum for Pte. Ltd.). Recommended: S$100,000–S$500,000 initial capital for MAS credibility. |
| 7 | **Constitution (M&AA)** | ☐ Pending | Adopt standard ACRA model constitution or draft custom. Must include provisions for reserved matters per Operating Charter. |
| 8 | **KYC / Identity Documents** | ☐ Pending | Passport copies, proof of address for all directors and shareholders. Corporate shareholder (SCV) requires Certificate of Incorporation, board resolution. |

| # | Requirement | Timeline | Notes |
|---|-------------|----------|-------|
| 1 | **Corporate Bank Account** | Week 1-2 post-incorporation | Standard Chartered Singapore is the natural banking partner. |

## 3. PSA License Decision: Standard vs Major Payment Institution

### 3.1 Payment Services Act 2019 (PSA) — License Tiers

The PSA defines two license tiers for payment service providers:

| Criterion | Standard Payment Institution (SPI) | Major Payment Institution (MPI) |
|-----------|-----------------------------------|--------------------------------|
| **Monthly transaction volume** | < S$3 million | ≥ S$3 million |
| **Daily e-money float** | < S$5 million | ≥ S$5 million |
| **Base capital requirement** | S$100,000 | S$250,000 |
| **Ongoing capital requirement** | S$100,000 or higher | S$250,000 or higher |
| **Safeguarding requirement** | Not required | Mandatory (trust account, guarantee, or insurance) |
| **Regulatory burden** | Lower compliance overhead | Full AML/CFT, technology risk, business conduct |
| **Application timeline** | 3-6 months | 6-12 months |

### 3.2 Relevant PSA Payment Services for AOA Phase 1

Under PSA 2019, payment services are categorised into seven types. AOA Phase 1 scope maps to:

| PSA Service Category | AOA Relevance | Phase |
|----------------------|---------------|-------|
| **Domestic money transfer service** | ✅ Primary — SGD transfers between AOA accounts | Phase 1 |
| **Cross-border money transfer service** | ⬜ Future — HKD/USD corridors | Phase 2+ |
| **Account issuance service** | ✅ Relevant — issuing AOA payment accounts | Phase 1 |
| **E-money issuance** | ⚠️ Possibly — if AOA balances qualify as e-money | Phase 1 (evaluate) |
| **Digital payment token (DPT)** | ⬜ Not applicable in Phase 1 | N/A |
| **Merchant acquisition** | ⬜ Not applicable | N/A |
| **Money-changing** | ⬜ Not applicable in Phase 1 | N/A |

### 3.3 Decision: Standard Payment Institution (SPI) for Phase 1

**Recommendation: Start with SPI license application.**

**Rationale:**

1. **Transaction Volume**: Phase 1 targets pilot customers with SGD domestic transfers. Monthly transaction volume will be well below S$3M threshold during sandbox and initial commercialisation.

2. **E-Money Float**: AOA agent account balances during Phase 1 pilot will not approach S$5M daily float. Conservative estimate: S$100K–S$500K during first 12 months.

3. **Lower Capital Requirement**: S$100,000 base capital vs S$250,000. Preserves runway for engineering investment.

4. **Faster Licensing**: SPI applications typically process in 3-6 months vs 6-12 months for MPI. Critical for time-to-market.

5. **Upgrade Path**: PSA explicitly allows upgrading from SPI → MPI when thresholds are crossed. This is a well-trodden path; no need to over-engineer licensing from Day 1.

6. **Sandbox Synergy**: MAS FinTech Regulatory Sandbox may relax certain requirements during the sandbox period, making SPI sufficient for validation.

**Upgrade Trigger**: When monthly volumes approach S$2M (67% of threshold) or daily float approaches S$3.5M, initiate MPI application process. Lead time: 6-9 months.

### 3.4 SPI Application Key Requirements

| Requirement | Detail |
|-------------|--------|
| **Base capital** | S$100,000 paid-up |
| **Permanent place of business** | Singapore office (registered address) |
| **Fit and proper directors** | Background checks, no criminal record, relevant experience |
| **AML/CFT framework** | Compliance officer, transaction monitoring, suspicious transaction reporting |
| **Technology risk management** | Per MAS Technology Risk Management Guidelines (TRM) |
| **Audit** | Annual audit by MAS-approved auditor |
| **Business plan** | 3-year projections, risk assessment, compliance programme |

- Regulatory license submissions (including MAS PSA)
- Brand/IP decisions
- Equity/cap table changes
- Strategic pivots

### Ring-Fencing Requirements

| Principle | Implementation |
|-----------|----------------|
| **Separate balance sheet** | AoaBank Pte. Ltd. maintains own accounts, not consolidated into SC Group |
| **Separate bank accounts** | Dedicated corporate accounts (Standard Chartered SG) |
| **Separate regulatory registrations** | Own ACRA UEN, own MAS license |
| **Independent audit** | Separate statutory audit (may use same auditor as SCV, separate engagement) |
| **Operational independence** | Own technology infrastructure, own compliance function |

## 5. Key Risks & Mitigations

| Risk | Severity | Mitigation |
|------|----------|------------|
| MAS rejects SPI application | High | Engage experienced PSA counsel early; MAS pre-application consultation |
| Director residency gap | Medium | Appoint SG-resident nominee director; expedite EP applications |
| E-money classification uncertainty | Medium | Seek MAS guidance on AOA account balance classification |
| ACRA name rejection | Low | Prepare 2-3 alternative names |
| Delayed bank account opening | Medium | Leverage SC relationship for expedited onboarding |
